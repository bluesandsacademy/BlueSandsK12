# Icons and copy must earn their place

This is a real product for Nigerian schools, not a demo. Copy and iconography that
read as machine-generated cost us credibility with head teachers and proprietors.

**Every icon must depict its subject.** `Mail` on a "Get in Touch" heading, `Video`
on a webinar badge, `Package` on an order summary. Never use `Sparkles`, `Zap`,
`Rocket`, `Wand2`, or ✨/🚀 as generic garnish meaning "this bit is exciting". Test:
if you cannot point at the noun in the adjacent text that the icon draws, delete the
icon. A badge with no icon beats a badge with a meaningless one. (Icons standing in
for an actual list item, as in `social-impact/approach.jsx`, are content, not garnish,
and this does not apply.)

**No em dashes in user-facing copy.** Use a comma, a colon, or two sentences.

**Avoid the stock LLM register.** No "Unlock", "Supercharge", "Elevate", "Seamless",
"Dive in", "Game-changing", "In today's fast-paced world". No "It's not just X, it's Y."
No three-fragment tricolons for rhythm. Write what the thing does, in the plainest
words that do the job.
